Part 1: Strings, Frets and Fingers


In guitar tablature each number thats annoted on the string represents the area of the fretboard that you play it on.

The numbers on a piece of tablature represent the fret numbers you will press down. Single numbers from left to right

are single note melodies. Numbers stacked vertally are called chords. Chords are notes played simultaneously.


Part 2: Standard Music vs Tablature


Tablature is annoted differently from standard music notion. Notice that there are only 5 lines in Standard music

but 6 lines in Tablature. The difference between the two is the fact that tab uses numbers to mark notes and standard

uses symbols as notes. The numbers on the side represent the number of guitar strings and the name of each string.


Part 3: Guitar Fretboard Layout


There are usually 22-24 frets on each guitar. One thing you should know is that the fretboat notes start over after the

12th fret. Reminder! the 6th strings is also called the Low E and the 1st string is called the High E string. The letters

next to the numbers are the names of each string on the guitar. This letter combination is known as standard E tuning.

Part 4: Single Notes and Chords


Single notes in guitar tab are offset and played separately, while chords are notes that are played

simultaneously (think strumming all 6 string). The 0 represents an Open note, meaning you play the string

with out pressing down on the string. Almost all tabs have open notes in them, whether in chords or singular.

Part 5: Scales in Tablature


Scales in Tablature are represented as a sequence of notes. A Scales that starts at the Low E string

and ends at the High E is called a Descending scale. A Scale that starts from the High E and works

up is called an Ascending scale. There are many different scales and modes represented in guitar tablature.
